It is a statutory procedure that allows landlords of commercial premises to recover rent arrears by seizing the tenant's goods and selling them. For commercial landlords who wish to recover rent arrears, Commercial Rent Arrears Recovery (CRAR) is an option. Check out this guide for more information.

As a result of the Corona virus Act 2020, Changes Will Be Made To CRAR

As a result of the Corona virus Act 2020, landlords cannot use the CRAR procedure if rent is not in arrears until their rent is in arrears. The level of arrears required during this period was much higher. The regular arrears threshold (a sum equal to seven days' rent) has been reinstated as of 26 March 2022.

CRAR: what is it and how does it work?

The Commercial Rent Arrears Recovery (CRAR) procedure allows landlords to recoup rent arrears by seizing the tenant's goods and selling them. A new commercial lease must comply with CRAR after 6 April 2014 and all existing leases must comply with CRAR as of that date. A landlord must notify the tenant of the enforcement of the CRAR seven days in advance. A Certificated Enforcement Agent may seize goods after the expiration of this period (through an open or unlocked door).

As a result of the Anti-Avoidance Provisions in place, landlords are prohibited from creating contracts that modify CRAR or other terms that will allow them to recover rent arrears by seizing goods as an alternative method of recovering rent arrears. A landlord and tenant can, however, agree that CRAR can be applied in a limited manner on the rental property.

Can CRAR be applied in a limited number of situations?

  • The CRAR applies only to commercial premises
  • Principal rent may only be recouped through CRAR - other sums (e.g. service charges and insurance premiums) cannot be recovered.
  • In order to conduct CRAR, Certified Enforcement Agents must follow statutory procedures (not other types of bailiffs).

Is CRAR available to everyone and what are the requirements?

It is generally not possible to use CRAR if your tenant remains in occupation of your commercial property. In addition, there must be a written lease. You will not be able to occupy the premises with just a contractual license.

  • Detailed information must be included in the tenant's notice
  • A specific method must be used to serve notice
  • From the date a notice of seizure is served (12 months), goods can be seized
  • A certain amount of time must pass before goods can be seized
  • An inventory must be prepared before seized goods can be seized.
  • After goods have been seized, they must be valued and must not be sold for seven days

When can CRAR not be used?

  • You cannot use CRAR if you meet the following criteria:
  • There is a mixed-use property or a residential property leased
  • Leases are no longer valid (subject to certain exceptions)
  • If a certain arrear cannot be calculated (e.g. turnover rents), then that arrear cannot be calculated with certainty.
